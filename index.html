Formulated and Founded by Syed Imon Rizvi — 2025

The Aligile Manifesto

Wisdom before Prescription (Taslīm → Yaqīn)

We begin with humility before reality. No practice has meaning without understanding its purpose.

We prefer knowledge with conviction over rituals without soul.

Conviction as Collective Purpose (Yaqīn)

True agility is powered by unshakable belief in the mission. Teams align their convictions with delivering value and serving people.

We prefer deep shared purpose over shallow compliance.

Truth over Assumptions (Tasdīq)

Every review and metric is an act of truth-seeking — courageous, evidence-based, and transparent.

We prefer honest verification over comforting illusions.

Public Promise as Accountability (Iqrār)

Our commitments are visible; boards and metrics are public acknowledgments — promises to each other and to society.

We prefer transparent acknowledgment over private intention.

Integrity in Completion (Adāʾ)

“Done” is not a checklist. It is the honorable discharge of our promises — quality with moral weight.

We prefer fulfillment with integrity over completion with shortcuts.

Meaningful Action (ʿAmal)

All inner work must lead to outward good. Our increments must create value and avoid harm.

We prefer meaningful action over empty output.

Principles of Aligile

1. Wisdom Before Prescription

We value understanding and reflection over blind adherence to methods.

“There is no wealth like wisdom.”

2. Consultation as Guidance

We make decisions through collective consultation, not individual decree.

“No support more reliable than consultation.”

3. Justice as the Foundation

We uphold fairness and balance in all actions, ensuring systems remain just and sustainable.

“Justice puts things in their places… justice is superior.”

4. Truth Over Assumption

We seek evidence and reflection before acting, verifying reality over chasing illusions.

“The tongue of the wise is behind his heart.”

5. Patience and Forbearance in Challenge

We respond to conflict and hardship with restraint, courage, and endurance.

“Forbearance is the bridle of the fool.”

6. Conviction Through Shared Purpose

We cultivate deep belief in our mission, giving strength and direction to our work.

“He who has several opinions understands the pitfalls.”

7. Accountability Through Public Promise

We declare our commitments openly, treating visibility as a covenant of trust.

“He who is content with his own opinion faces danger.”

8. Integrity in Fulfillment

“Done” is not a checklist; it is the honorable discharge of our promises.

“Victory is by determination.”

9. Meaningful Action, Not Empty Output

We strive for outcomes that matter, carrying value and resilience in changing times.

“Through change of circumstances the mettle of men is known.”

10. Endurance Over Fragility

We pursue steady, patient progress rather than fragile bursts of speed.

“Endurance braves calamities.”

11. Humility Over Arrogance

We reject greed and pride, building agility on service and humility.

“He who adopts greed as a habit devalues himself.”

12. Systems Above Ego

We design for justice and sustainability, not personal gain or ego-driven control.

“Justice… is superior.”
